- Common Frameworks for Ludology: MDA, Formal

- MDA: Mechanics, Dynamics, & Aesthetics

- Proposed at the Game Developers Conference 2001

- Best known framework for game analysis

- Three Elements:

- Mechanics: The particular components of the game at the level of data representation and algorithms

- Dynamics: the runtime behavior of the mechanics acting on player inputs and each others' outputs over time

- Aesthetics: The desirable emotional response

- Designers go in the order: Aesthetics > Dynamics > Mechanics

- Players experience the reverse order: Mechanics > Dynamics > Aesthetics

- Example: Snakes and Ladders board game

- Aesthetic - experiencing hope to win the game

- Dynamics

- Formal, Dramatic, and Dynamic Elements

- Formal elements - the elements that make games different from other forms of media or interaction. How games are different from books, movies, etc

- A games ceases to be a game if the formal elements are removed

- Seven formal elements of games:

-1) Player Interaction Pattern - How do players interact

- Single-Player, One-on-one, Team versus team, Multilateral, Unilateral, Cooperative play

-2) Objective - What are the players trying to achieve in the game? How do we know when has someone won the game?

-3) Rules - Limit the players' actions by telling them what they may and may not do in the game

- Many rules are explicit, but others are implicitly understood

-4) Procedures - Actions taken by the player in the game

- Rule tells the player what they can do, procedure dictated by that rule is the actual action

- Often defined by the interaction of a number of rules

-5) Resources - elements that have value in the game

- Money, health

-6) Boundaries - Where does the game end and reality begins?

- This concept is now known as the "Magic Circle"

-7) Outcome - How did the game end?

- Both final and incremental outcomes

- Chess: One player wins and the other loses

- Dramatic elements - the story or narrative of the game, including the premise.

- Make the rules and resources more understandable

- Give players greater emotional investments in the game

- Three dramatic elements of games:

-1) Premise - the basic story of the game world (theme)

-2) Character - The individuals around whom the story revolves

-3) Story - the plot of the game

- Dynamic Elements - the game in motion. Include things like strategy, behavior, relationships between entities
